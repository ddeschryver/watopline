Kentucky State School Choice Mar 19, 2026 WDRB
🔥 Action 3

Kentucky School Choice Bill Becomes Law After Senate Overrides Governor Veto

House Bill 1 — which gives Kentucky families access to the federal Education Freedom Tax Credit program — became law after the Republican-controlled Senate voted to override Governor Andy Beshear's veto. The override follows a contentious multi-year debate: the state Supreme Court struck down a 2022 charter school funding measure in February, and voters rejected a 2024 ballot measure that would have allowed public dollars to flow to private schools. HB 1 sidesteps those constraints by routing access through a federal program.

W/A Perspective The bottom line is that all states are going to join. If a governor doesn't, a resident can still contribute to an SGO in another state — so of course they will, after the politics play out.

Top 5 K–12 Funding Disruptions of Trump's Second Term

A W/A Research review of early second-term federal actions identifies the defining disruptions to K–12 finance: OMB's $6.2 billion formula fund withholding, ESSER reimbursement freezes, and a discretionary grant pause that created broad uncertainty. Iowa Bill Calls for Study of School EdTech Use — A Bellwether for the Nation

Iowa lawmakers are advancing legislation to study the impact of school-issued technology on students' focus and learning. The Senate unanimously approved a proposal directing state education and health agencies to convene a working group of experts and parents and issue recommendations. The measure is positioned as a moderate alternative to a stricter House bill that would cap elementary classroom screen time — reflecting a growing national debate over how best to balance technology use in K–12 settings.

W/A Perspective This is one of the hottest issues in education right now, and it's likely that most states will settle on a solution like the one Iowa is approaching.

California District Budget Mar 17, 2026 Oak Grove School District
🔥 Action 3

California District Budget Forecast: Flat Revenues, Rising Costs, Persistent Deficits

Oak Grove Elementary School District's board budget presentation is a template for what is happening across California right now. Revenues are largely flat near-term but projected to decline in outer years as one-time funds disappear and enrollment drops — each departing student representing roughly $13,000 in lost per-pupil funding. Expenditure growth in special education services, staffing, insurance, utilities, and negotiated salaries is producing deficits in the $10–12 million range, requiring ongoing reserve drawdowns to remain solvent.

Washington District Budget Mar 17, 2026 Peninsula School District
🔥 Action 3

Washington State Funding Cuts Will Compress District Budgets Beginning 2026–27

A Peninsula School District board update details the downstream impact of Washington SB 6260: the legislation cuts Transitional Kindergarten funding, reduces Running Start enrollment, freezes national board bonus cost-of-living adjustments, and changes transportation depreciation rules. Over the past decade, education's share of the state budget has declined from 52% to 42%. A board member summarized the sentiment: "The legislature had a chance to strengthen public education this year. Instead it cut support in key areas."

Massachusetts State SPED Mar 16, 2026 Boston Globe
🔥 Action 3

Massachusetts Special Education Teachers Are Leaving in Droves — and the Crisis Is Spreading

Massachusetts is facing a severe special education teacher shortage, with roughly one-third of instructors leaving their positions each year. Budget cuts, difficult working conditions, and a shrinking pipeline of new educators are driving the exodus. As the share of students with disabilities continues to rise, districts increasingly rely on underqualified or emergency-licensed staff, disrupting services and student progress. Experts warn the state is not meeting demand and call for urgent action on recruitment, training, and retention.

W/A Perspective The importance of special education continues to rise, but the capacity to serve students continues to struggle. This is going to be an all-hands-on-deck matter very soon.
