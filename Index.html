Top 5 K-12 Funding Disruptions of Trump's Second Term

A W/A Research review of early second-term federal actions identifies the defining disruptions to K-12 finance: OMB's $6.2 billion formula fund withholding, ESSER reimbursement freezes, and a discretionary grant pause that created broad uncertainty. Department of Education staffing reductions and program shifts added further instability. Despite the turbulence, FY2026 appropriations ultimately preserved core funding — shifting the longer-term conversation toward structural challenges including enrollment decline and rising operating costs.

Washington Democrats Settle on Plan to Patch $79.4 Billion Strained Budget

Washington's budget framework includes targeted education reductions: Transition to Kindergarten funding is cut by $27 million — potentially eliminating up to one-third of seats — and a planned $100 per-student increase in local effort assistance has been canceled, saving $25 million. Higher education avoids deeper cuts through a $240 million fund transfer, though administrative cost reductions are required, reflecting a broad effort to close budget gaps while limiting the impact on schools.
