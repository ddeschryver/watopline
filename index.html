Colorado lawmakers cut newer programs to close $1.2 billion budget shortfall. The Teacher Recruitment Education and Preparation Program was created in 2021 through legislation that passed the Senate unanimously. The law set up two initiatives to support students preparing to become teachers and provided high schools with just under $11,000 per participating student each year. According to Joint Budget Committee Chair Rep. Emily Sirota, D‑Denver, the program currently serves 193 students. The Joint Budget Committee voted to reduce those payments to just over $7,000 per student for the 2026‑27 fiscal year, after which the program will end. Students already enrolled in the program will be allowed to finish, but no new students will be allowed to join. The change is expected to save $1.6 million in the 2026‑27 fiscal year, money that would come from the state education fund.
