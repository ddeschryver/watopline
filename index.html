South Carolina - State Level
South Carolina H. 3197 — Workforce Readiness (2026) H. 3197 strengthens workforce readiness requirements in South Carolina schools, aligning K–12 education with career and technical pathways. The bill aims to expand industry credential opportunities and connect students to in-demand sectors, addressing the state's skilled labor gap through structured pre-workforce programming. The law sets a statewide goal of 60% workforce credential attainment by 2032, requires high school seniors to complete FAFSA before graduation, mandates literacy and math remediation courses for underprepared seniors, and directs the Department of Employment and Workforce to publish free online data on the economic value of college majors beginning July 2026.
